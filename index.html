BOXING HISTORY

Boxing, as we know it today, cannot be understood without its history. Evidence shows that during Ancient Greece, it was introduced as an Olympic sport called Pygmachia.

Later, gladiators used it in the Coliseum for death matches, wearing leather and metal gloves, which often ended in fatalities.

In the contemporary era, with the prohibition of sword duels and the rise of unregulated bare-knuckle fighting, the first boxing regulations were introduced in 1743.

The modern rules we know today were established by the Marquis of Queensberry, who introduced padded gloves and required fighters to expose their torsos in professional settings. In 1882, the last bare-knuckle heavyweight fight took place in Mississippi.

Modern boxing emerged in the early 20th century, with the creation of weight classes such as welter, heavyweight, light heavyweight, and lightweight. One of the last changes to the sport was the reduction of rounds from 15 to 12 due to the increase in injuries caused by physical fatigue in the final rounds.

BOXING BASICS

1.Jab:
A quick, straight punch with the lead hand. Used to keep distance and set up other punches.
2. Cross:
A powerful, straight punch with the rear hand. Delivered after a jab.
3. Hook:
A curved punch aimed at the side of the opponent’s head or body. Thrown with either hand.
4. Uppercut:
A vertical punch directed upwards, targeting the opponent’s chin.
5.Overhand
A looping punch delivered from above, usually with the rear hand

    • BOXING RULES

    1. The only method of attack is punching with a clenched fist and you may not strike below the belt, in the kidneys or the back of your opponents head or neck.


    2. You cannot use the ropes for leverage.


    3. You cannot hit an opponent when they are down.


    4. A boxer hit with a low blow can take five minutes to recover.


    5. If an unintentional foul (such as a clash of heads) ends the fight before four rounds are completed this is a “no contest”, from the fifth onwards the decision goes to the judge’s cards and is either a technical decision for either fighter or a technical draw.
